SSED

SSED (Surgical Survivability Evaluation Device) is a smart medical device designed to calculate your anaerobic threshold by gathering accurate pulse and breathing rates. In hospital, accurate pulse and breathing rates (crucial biological data) of the patient must be gathered before they undergo surgery to calculate their anaerobic threshold which will test their chance of recovery after the surgery. It can also be used at home/personally. I have designed a device that can replace the complex and expensive Surgical Survivability Evaluation Device currently used in the North Shore Hospital to a simplified, cost-effective device that can accurately collect critical biological data (breathing and pulse rate) to access the survivability of a patient before going through a surgery.
